Lechon

Home

Description

Lechon is a signature Filipino dish. It involves grilling a whole piglet over a spit roast for a long time. With it's crispy texture and juiciness, this dish is popular for all kinds of celebrations such as birthdays, weddings, and parties.

Ingredients

  • An entire piglet
  • Apple
  • Lemon grass
  • Onions
  • Lots of salt and pepper
  • Garlic cloves
  • Oil

Steps

  1. Properly prepare the piglet and spit. This involves gutting the pig and making sure it won't fall off the spit roast, as it will spin several hundred times during cooking.
  2. Leave a cavity on the pig starting from the ventral side, just under the base of its head, all the way down to the bottom part of its stomach region.
  3. Prepare your seasonings. Grind and chop the onions and garlic cloves in a pot with oil, and shove them, along with the lemon grass, inside the cavity you made.
  4. Close your cavity using cotton threads or butcher's twine. Stainless steel strings also work
  5. Attach your pig to the spit roast and leave it spinning until it's your desired color.
  6. Take out it out of the roast and put an apple in the piglet's mouth for presentation
